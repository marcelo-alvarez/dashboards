TL;DR

  • A full box now runs end-to-end at production scale and lean. The 8-GPU (2-node) MPI path

populates a full AbacusSummit box — 39.1M galaxies — applies the lean contract, and writes a single

parallel-HDF5 file in 101 s wall. Output is 3.7 GB vs 37 GB before (10×).

  • A single GPU is not enough — a full box OOMs on 1 (and on 4) GPUs in the subhalo-generation step

(~40–49 GiB/rank); the proven config is 2 nodes × 4 GPU = 8 ranks (~750k halos/rank). No code

regression — just the real memory footprint.

  • The Roman selection works and looks physical. Applying the v0 calibration + an HLSS-like flux cut

to the existing catalogs gives a sensible n(z), peaking at z≈1.1 (~1.2×10⁻³ (h/Mpc)³).

  • A3 (full v0 production) is feasible well within the deadline: ~630 GPU-node-hours total; at

64–128 boxes in parallel that is ~2.5–5 hours wall. On-the-fly outputs keep the footprint to

~100–150 GB (vs 40 TB if we kept full lean catalogs, vs 787 TB original).

Production timing & scale

  • Per box (z=1.1, 8 GPU): 101 s wall incl. allocation/compile; populate+write ~60–80 s.
  • Boxes for v0: 1883 realizations × 6 in-band redshifts (0.5–2.0) = 11,298 box-populations.
  • Compute: ~630 node-hours. With 64 concurrent boxes (128 nodes) ≈ 5 h wall; 128 concurrent ≈ 2.5 h.
  • Out-of-band redshifts (0.2/0.25/0.3, 2.5/3.0) are not Hα-calibrated (Hα leaves the Roman band) —

excluded from v0, as planned.

On-the-fly footprint

A full lean catalog is 3.7 GB; keeping all 11,298 would be ~40 TB. The on-the-fly contract avoids

that: in the populate job, after calibration + Roman selection, write only the data vector (the

covariance statistic) + the small Roman-selected subcatalog (pos, vel, L_cal for the ~10⁴–10⁵

selected galaxies; ~5–10 MB/box), and discard the full catalog. Total ≈ 100–150 GB.
