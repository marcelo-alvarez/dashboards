Diffsky input validation — GSMF, sSFR–M*, stellar–halo mass (z = 1.4)

roman-covariance · perlmutter-marcelos-claude-code · read-only diagnostic vs the empirical literature

What this is

The mock galaxies are populated from Diffsky input catalogs (diffmah/diffstar SFHs →

logsm_t_obs, logssfr_t_obs, logmp_t_obs). Diffsky shares lineage with UniverseMachine

(Hearin co-authors both), so it should reproduce UM-like galaxy–halo statistics. This report

tests Marcelo's hypothesis that the SFR scaling diverges from the empirical (UM) expectation,

by measuring the input statistics at the fiducial redshift z = 1.4 (one box, 34.5M

galaxies) and overlaying the published literature. **Read-only — the production catalogs and

the running pipeline are untouched.**

Conventions (ruled out before reading any divergence)

  • h = 0.6774 (DSPS DEFAULT_COSMOLOGY, the cosmology the catalogs were generated with).

The box is 500 Mpc/h → number densities use the physical volume (500/h)³ Mpc³.

  • M\* and sSFR are physical / h-free (diffstar integrates the SFH in M⊙). Mpeak

carries the AbacusSummit Msun/h normalization → divided by h for the SMHM x-axis.

  • IMF: DSPS default (Chabrier-like) vs Chabrier (UM/Tomczak) / Kroupa (Speagle) — offsets

≤ 0.05 dex, not applied. SFR timescale: diffstar sSFR is instantaneous; the observed

UV+IR sSFRs are ~100-Myr averaged (diffstar SFHs are smooth, so the mismatch is small) —

flagged, not corrected.

  • Halo-mass definition: the Abacus CompaSO mass fed to diffsky is not identically UM's

Mpeak (Bryan–Norman vir.) — a known cross-definition offset, flagged below.

1. Galaxy stellar mass function (GSMF)

Φ(M\*) measured from the z=1.4 inputs (physical volume) against Tomczak+2014 (ZFOURGE,

1.25<z<1.5 — one of the datasets UM itself was calibrated to, and a clean z-bracket match).

The grey band marks the low-mass region made incomplete by the 10¹⁰ M⊙/h halo-mass cut applied

at generation.

2. Specific star-formation rate vs stellar mass (the main-sequence test)

This is the direct test of the hypothesis. Median sSFR(M\*) from diffsky (red, with the

16–84% band) against the analytic Speagle+2014 and Schreiber+2015 main sequences and the

observed Tomczak+2015 / Whitaker+2014 sSFRs at z≈1.4.

3. Stellar–halo mass relation (centrals)

Median M\*(Mpeak) for central galaxies (upid == −1) against **UniverseMachine /

Behroozi+2019** (true-median centrals, z=1.4; generated from the UM DR1 parameter release).

Diagnostic — where/how diffsky diverges (measured, 1 box: 34.5M galaxies, 5.78M centrals)

  • Main sequence (the headline): the diffsky sSFR is low and, at low/intermediate mass,

too flat. The measured median is log sSFR(M\*=10¹⁰) = −9.24, vs Speagle+2014 = −8.79

and Schreiber+2015 = −8.93 at z=1.4 — i.e. ≈ 0.45 dex low at 10¹⁰ M⊙, growing to

~0.6–0.9 dex at M\* ≲ 10⁹·⁵ where the diffsky sequence is flat while the observed one still

rises. Over M\* ≲ 10¹⁰ the diffsky sSFR is nearly constant ⇒ SFR ∝ M\* (slope ≈ 1) vs the

observed ≈ 0.72, so the deficit is worst at low mass. This is consistent with Marcelo's

suspected SFR-scaling divergence.

  • A sharp high-mass downturn, not a flat extrapolation: above M\* ≈ 10¹⁰·⁵ the median sSFR

drops steeply (−9.70 at 10¹⁰·⁹, −10.21 at 10¹¹·²), so the global slope over 10⁹·⁵–10¹¹ is

≈ −0.31 (comparable to the observed −0.28). The earlier "flat everywhere / no quenching"

read is corrected by the figure: the bulk (≈ 98% of galaxies, number-weighted and dominated

by M\* < 10¹⁰) sits in one narrow star-forming sequence within log sSFR ∈ [−9.6, −9.0] with no

distinct quiescent mode, but a real high-mass quenching turn-down is present at the

massive end (where galaxies are rare).

  • The mc_galpop M⊙-vs-M⊙/h units lead is NOT confirmed by these figures: both the GSMF and the

SMHM (centrals) broadly match observations / UM — the SMHM tracks Behroozi+2019 over

Mpeak ≈ 10¹¹–10¹²·⁵ and falls only ~0.2–0.35 dex low at the very massive end. A ~0.17 dex (1/h)

over-statement of halo mass feeding mc_galpop would shift M\*(Mpeak) by ~0.17 dex, which is

not seen here. So the units inconsistency is at most a secondary effect to check in the code,

not the demonstrated cause; the clean signal is that **M\* and M\*(Mhalo) are about right

while the sSFR normalization is ~0.45 dex low** — the divergence is in the star-formation

scaling, not the stellar-mass or stellar–halo mapping.

Scope: this is one z=1.4 box (rehearsal100_v1.0 inputs; the diffsky SFHs are identical to

the production inputs for this read-only diagnostic). Extending the same panels across the other 11

redshifts (the validation plan's low/high-z emphasis) will show whether the sSFR deficit grows

toward the extremes, as hypothesized.
