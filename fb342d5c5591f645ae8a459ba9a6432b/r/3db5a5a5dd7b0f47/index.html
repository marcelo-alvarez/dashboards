Recalibration diagnostic (z = 1.4) — three luminosities × two (M*, SFR) sets

roman-covariance · perlmutter-marcelos-claude-code · one realization, diagnostic only

Question

If we first recalibrate M\* to the observed GSMF by an Mpeak-dependent factor (to "get

back" the GSMF, hopefully without breaking the Behroozi SMHM), and then re-scale SFR by an

M\*-dependent factor so the median sSFR–M\* lands on Schreiber+15, how does the

SFR↔L_Hα relation move, and does the LF-pinned line luminosity change? One z=1.4 box

(34,513,407 galaxies, 5,784,042 centrals). **Diagnostic only — diffsky parameters are NOT tuned;

the production calibration is unchanged (stays L_out = LF-only abundance matching).**

Definitions

Three luminosities (Hα; L = SFR/7.9e-42, linear — confirmed in code and verified equal

to the catalog l_halpha):

  • L_in — raw, no abundance matching.
  • L_out — single-realization AM(L_in → Nagaraj+2023 Hα LF). *Current production method.*
  • L_out_gsmf — single-realization AM(L_in_scaled → Nagaraj), with `L_in_scaled =

SFR_scaled/7.9e-42` (the LF match applied *after* the GSMF+SFR recalibration).

Two (M\*, SFR) sets: raw diffsky (M*, SFR), and (M*, SFR)_scaled:

M*_scaled = M*·f1(Mpeak) (f1 abundance-matches M\* to the Tomczak+14 GSMF as a function of

Mpeak, preserving the diffsky scatter); SFR_scaled = SFR·f2(M*_scaled) (f2 sets the median of

SFR/M*_scaled onto Schreiber+15 at z=1.4, preserving scatter).

Results

1. The two recalibrations land on their targets by construction. The scaled GSMF tracks the

Tomczak+14 fit to ≤0.04 dex over 10⁹·⁵–10¹¹, and the scaled median sSFR–M\* sits exactly on

Schreiber+15. In the complete regime (M\*>10⁹·⁵) the median factors are f1 ≈ -0.34 dex

(M\* *lowered* — the raw diffsky GSMF is ~0.2–0.3 dex too *high*) and f2 ≈ +0.08 dex.

Notably f2 is small: because step 1 lowers M\*, the diffsky sSFR = SFR/M\* rises most of the way

to Schreiber on its own, so the second step only tops it up. The order of operations matters.

2. Fixing the GSMF *does* mildly break Behroozi. The raw diffsky central SMHM tracks

UM/Behroozi+19 well, but the (M\*)_scaled central SMHM falls ~0.2–0.3 dex below Behroozi at

logMpeak ≈ 11.5–12 (converging by logMpeak ≈ 13). This is not a fitting artifact: it is the

GSMF↔SMHM tension in this catalog — diffsky's M\*(Mpeak) already matches Behroozi, yet its

halo population is over-abundant at fixed Mpeak (83% satellites; and the CompaSO mass scale

differs from the Rockstar Mpeak UM was calibrated on), so its GSMF is ~0.2–0.3 dex too high.

You cannot push the GSMF down (lower M\* at fixed Mpeak) without pulling the central SMHM off

Behroozi by the same amount. (Answer to "hopefully not breaking Behroozi": it breaks it, mildly,

and the break *is* the GSMF excess re-expressed.)

3. The LF is unchanged; the per-galaxy luminosity moves only at the bright end. L_out and

L_out_gsmf are both pinned to the same Nagaraj LF (Row-1 right panel — they overlap on the

target). Because the LF is pinned, the only way per-galaxy L can move is the M\*-dependence of

f1/f2 reordering galaxies in L_in (a *constant* SFR boost would leave L_out_gsmf ≡ L_out).

That reordering is negligible for faint galaxies but **grows toward the bright,

Roman-relevant end: log₁₀(L_out_gsmf/L_out) is -0.13 dex (median) at L_out>10⁴²**

(16–84% [-0.22, +0.03], N=150,795) and **-0.25 dex at

L_out>10⁴²·⁵** — the recalibration *demotes the brightest emitters*. This is the

covariance-relevant change: it moves the L–M\*/L–Mhalo correlations, not the luminosity

function.

4. The SFR↔L panel (the new quantity). L_in vs SFR is the fixed linear Kennicutt line

(L=SFR/7.9e-42). L_out vs SFR and L_out_gsmf vs SFR_scaled are the abundance-matching remaps —

flatter than linear, since AM compresses the luminosity range onto Nagaraj. In the complete

regime the net L_in_scaled/L_in shift is small (~-0.04 dex) — the f2 SFR boost nearly

cancels because step 1 already raised sSFR; the large all-galaxy median (+0.5 dex) is dominated

by sub-completeness galaxies where the GSMF is extrapolated below the Tomczak data.

L_out_gsmf-vs-SFR_scaled is offset from L_out-vs-SFR by that x-axis SFR_scaled boost and the

bright-end reordering on the y-axis.

Scope

One realization, diagnostic only; scripts in the gitignored local/

(recal_experiment.py). Production calibration unchanged. diffsky parameters not tuned.

Below the GSMF completeness limit (M\*≲10⁹·⁵) the M\* abundance-matching extrapolates the

observed GSMF, so the sub-completeness factors are not interpreted.
