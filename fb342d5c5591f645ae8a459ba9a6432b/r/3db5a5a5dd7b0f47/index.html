Recalibration diagnostic (z = 1.4) — calibrated to UM/Behroozi+2019

roman-covariance · perlmutter-marcelos-claude-code · iteration 2, one realization, diagnostic only

Question

Recalibrate M\* to the observed GSMF (by an Mpeak-dependent factor) and SFR to the

observed sSFR–M\* relation, then trace how it propagates to the Hα line luminosity. **Single

self-consistent reference = UM/Behroozi+2019** (its GSMF, sSFR(M\*), and M\*(Mpeak) are fit

together): UM is the target for both recalibration steps and the only reference curve on the

GSMF / M\*–Mpeak / sSFR–M\* panels. Nagaraj+2023 stays as the Hα-LF target. One z=1.4 box

(34,513,407 galaxies, 5,784,042 centrals). **Diagnostic only — diffsky parameters are NOT tuned;

the production calibration is unchanged (stays L_out = LF-only abundance matching).**

Method

  • f1 (GSMF): rank abundance-match the diffsky halos' Mpeak to the UM GSMF using **n(>Mpeak)

↔ n(>M\*)** (anchored at the massive end), so (M*)_scaled lands on the UM GSMF by construction.

  • f2 (SFR): SFR_scaled = SFR·f2(M*_scaled) sets the median sSFR onto the UM sSFR(M\*)

relation (preserving each galaxy's scatter).

  • Three luminosities (Hα; L = SFR/7.9e-42, linear, verified == catalog l_halpha):

L_in (raw), L_out (AM(L_in→Nagaraj); *production method*), L_out_gsmf

(AM(L_in_scaled→Nagaraj), L_in_scaled = SFR_scaled/7.9e-42). Both L_out and L_out_gsmf use the

production abundance-matching routine (n(>L)).

Results

1. Both recalibrations land on the UM targets. (M*)_scaled reproduces the UM GSMF and the

recalibrated median sSFR–M\* sits on the UM sSFR(M\*) relation. Complete-regime (M\*>10⁹·⁵) median

factors: f1 ≈ -0.24 dex and f2 ≈ +0.05 dex.

2. The SMHM panel is now a halo-mass-scale diagnostic. UM's GSMF and SMHM are mutually

consistent, so matching M\* to the UM GSMF should reproduce the UM SMHM iff the Abacus halo

abundance / mass definition matches Bolshoi-Planck's. The residual: (M*)_scaled centrals sit

~-0.15 dex from UM/Behroozi at logMpeak ≈ 11.5–12. That offset is not a recalibration

flaw — it is the Abacus(CompaSO)-vs-BP(Rockstar-Mpeak) halo-mass-scale / little-h mismatch read

through the GSMF.

3. The LF is pinned; per-galaxy L moves only at the bright end. L_out and L_out_gsmf both stay

on the Nagaraj LF across the full L range (no high-L truncation), so per-galaxy L moves only

via the M\*-dependent f1/f2 reordering of L_in (a constant SFR boost would give

L_out_gsmf ≡ L_out). The reordering is negligible faint and grows bright: log₁₀(L_out_gsmf/L_out)

median -0.09 dex at L_out>10⁴² (16–84% [-0.24, +0.17], N=150,795)

and -0.08 dex at L_out>10⁴²·⁵. This is the covariance-relevant change — it moves the

L–M\*/L–Mhalo correlations, not the luminosity function.

4. The SFR↔L panel (the new quantity). L_in vs SFR is the linear Kennicutt line; L_out and

L_out_gsmf are the AM remaps (compressed onto Nagaraj). Net complete-regime L_in shift ≈

-0.02 dex.

Scope

One realization, diagnostic only; scripts in the gitignored local/ (recal_experiment.py,

build_um_targets.py). Production calibration unchanged; diffsky parameters not tuned.
