diffsky→UM at z=1.409 — GSMF self-consistency + mean sSFR (distribution + SFRD preserved)

Iteration 5 (this report)

Keeps report-9's accepted matching method unchanged (f1: Mpeak→UM GSMF; f2: conditional sSFR

quantile match). Three additions: (1) resolve the Tomczak-vs-UM GSMF offset at z=1.409 with a

multi-redshift UM interpolation; (2) plot the Tomczak points quoted self-consistently at

z=1.409; (3) show the mean sSFR(M\*) (not the all-galaxy median) against the data.

Infrastructure added: um_zinterp.py builds one interpolator of the UM/Behroozi+2019 model

data products (observed-SM GSMF, mean sSFR, cosmic SFRD) in (logM\*, z) from every DR1 snapshot

(178 scale factors), so the UM targets and data comparisons are evaluable at any z, not just 1.409.

Question

Match the diffsky catalog SFRs to the UniverseMachine (UM/Behroozi+2019) catalog so that **both

the overall SFR distribution and the total star-formation-rate density (SFRD) are preserved** —

the SFRD must reproduce UM at any redshift, not just the median main sequence. Then show the

median and mean sSFR in bins of M\* for each catalog. One z=1.4 box (diffsky 34.5M galaxies,

500 Mpc/h; UM 17.1M galaxies at a=0.41506 → z=1.409). Diagnostic only — diffsky parameters are

NOT tuned; the production calibration is unchanged.

Method — two abundance-matching steps, nothing fit

  • f1 (stellar mass): rank abundance-match the diffsky halos' Mpeak to the UM GSMF,

n(>Mpeak) ↔ n(>M\*), so the scaled M\* distribution reproduces the UM number density per mass.

  • f2 (SFR) — conditional quantile matching of sSFR at fixed M\*: within each 0.1-dex M\* bin,

map each diffsky galaxy's sSFR percentile onto UM's sSFR distribution at that percentile (UM's

per-bin CDF stored as a 1001-point quantile grid). This imposes UM's entire conditional

P(sSFR | M\*) — median, mean, scatter, and quenched fraction — not a single summary shift.

Because f1 fixes the M\* distribution and f2 fixes P(sSFR | M\*), the recalibrated joint

(M\*, SFR) distribution matches UM, so the overall SFR distribution and the integrated

SFRD = ∫ φ(M\*) ⟨SFR|M\*⟩ dM\* both reproduce UM by construction.

Why not a simpler shift. A *median* shift (the previous f2) preserves neither the mean nor the

SFRD once the sSFR distribution is scattered/quenched; a *mean* shift preserves the SFRD but

flattens the distribution shape (wrong scatter, wrong quenched fraction). Matching the conditional

CDF gets both at once.

(1)+(2) Tomczak vs the UM GSMF at z=1.409 — the offset is NOT redshift

Quoting both sides self-consistently at z=1.409 (UM model SMF in observed stellar masses from the

multi-z interpolator; Tomczak's 1.25<z<1.5 points Leja-evolved to z=1.409 from the bin's

volume-weighted effective z = 1.377):

  • The redshift correction is negligible and wrong-signed. The bin's effective z (1.377)

is *below* 1.409, so evolving the points to 1.409 *lowers* φ (≤ 0.016 dex) — the opposite

of what is needed to lift Tomczak onto UM. Redshift quoting cannot be the cause.

  • What remains is a flat normalization offset. UM sits +0.097 dex above Tomczak,

essentially mass-independent (rms only 0.019 dex over 9 < logM\* < 11.1). A

mass-independent vertical offset is the signature of a normalization difference, not redshift

(which would be mass-dependent) and not a stellar-mass-scale offset (which would tilt with mass).

  • Cause: the single-survey (ZFOURGE, 316 arcmin²) normalization vs the multi-survey compilation

UM/Behroozi+2019 is fit to — i.e. cosmic variance + survey systematics. ZFOURGE sits ~0.1 dex

low. Tomczak's published errors are Poisson+SED only (1.4σ from UM); adding a cosmic

-variance term σ_cv=0.1 dex (standard for a pencil-beam survey, omitted from the catalog

errors) brings the points to 0.8σ — they overlap the UM fitting function naturally.

Leja+2015 — a smooth fit to the *same* ZFOURGE data — evaluated at z=1.409 threads the raw Tomczak

points and lies the same ~0.1 dex below the UM model SMF, independently confirming the offset is in

the ZFOURGE normalization, not in the redshift treatment.

(3) Mean sSFR(M\*) — tracks the data; avoids the all-galaxy-median dive

The right panel now shows the mean sSFR (the SFRD-relevant statistic). The matched diffsky mean

equals UM by construction; at logM\*≈10–11 the UM/matched mean (-8.94 →

-9.60) sits within ~0.1 dex of the star-forming data, whereas the all-galaxy

median dives to -11.2 at logM\*=11 (quenched majority). The mean is the

correct comparison to the (star-forming) data and to the SFRD.
